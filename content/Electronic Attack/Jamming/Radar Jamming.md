# Overview
Radar jamming is a specialized form of electronic attack that focuses on preventing enemy radar systems from detecting, tracking, or targeting friendly forces. Rather than physically destroying a radar installation, electronic jamming interferes with the radio waves used by the radar until the operator receives little or no useful information. This allows friendly aircraft, ships, or ground forces to maneuver with a reduced chance of detection while maintaining the element of surprise (Stealth Veil, n.d.).

# Types of Radar Jamming
Radar jamming can be performed in several different ways depending on the mission. Noise jamming overwhelms the radar receiver with radio frequency energy, making legitimate targets difficult to identify. Spot jamming focuses on a single radar frequency for maximum effectiveness, while barrage jamming spreads energy across multiple frequencies to interfere with several radar systems simultaneously. More advanced forms of radar jamming also include deception techniques that create false targets or alter the apparent location of real ones, forcing the enemy to make incorrect tactical decisions (Stealth Veil, n.d.; Lichtman et al., 2016).

# Military Uses
Radar jamming is commonly used during air operations, naval missions, and electronic warfare campaigns to improve survivability. Fighter aircraft often carry onboard jamming pods that interfere with enemy air-defense radars while escort aircraft provide protection for larger formations. Ground-based electronic warfare systems can also jam surveillance radars, reducing an adversary’s ability to detect troop movements or incoming attacks. When combined with deception and other electronic attack techniques, radar jamming significantly increases operational flexibility while reducing the effectiveness of enemy sensors (Stealth Veil, n.d.; Lichtman et al., 2016).
